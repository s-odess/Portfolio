05

Perspective

Scale is a legitimate business imperative. Do it. But don't mistake it for oversight, and don't assume engineers can provide what oversight actually requires.

An engineer's job is to build and maintain systems — not to audit them from outside, not to represent the perspective of the people those systems affect. Most users aren't engineers. Most failure modes aren't visible from inside the system. The gap between statistically plausible output and accurate output doesn't close with scale — it becomes harder to see, because outputs get more fluent and evaluation instruments share the same blind spots as the models they're measuring.

I've measured this in production. The oversight requirement is structural, not transitional. It doesn't diminish as models improve — it changes shape. The organizations building for that now are the ones that won't be surprised later.
