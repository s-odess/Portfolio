Independent LLM Audit Under Production Constraints

When production LLMs entered a high-stakes publishing workflow, the existing quality rubric (designed for human analysts) produced inaccurate evaluations of model output. No systematic QA infrastructure existed for the new pipeline.

I built one independently. Using macOS Shortcuts and Bash scripts, I designed and executed an adversarial auditing methodology that captured and catalogued over 1,000 model outputs across factual inaccuracy, editorial violations, and privacy failures, without dedicated infrastructure, and without being asked.

The audit surfaced error rates exceeding 10%, peaking at 12%. I presented the findings to organizational leadership; they directly informed subsequent QA calibration processes. My own quality assessment scores improved in the immediately following evaluation cycle, confirming that the error source had been correctly identified, not just described.

I then extended the same methodology to two additional use cases: ongoing output verification of LLM-generated notifications against primary government source data, and LLM-assisted deduplication of over 100 redundant notification series generated from unverified user-generated content where no authoritative narrative context existed. I authored a formal written business case assessing the expected impact and risks of a new automation workflow, presented it to management under critical questioning, and secured approval to deploy it.

The project demonstrated something that matters beyond Citizen: lightweight, human-driven audit infrastructure surfaces failure modes that automated pipelines are structurally unable to detect, and doing so has measurable operational impact.

06

Perspective

Scaling AI systems and honestly evaluating them require different orientations. Production environments are optimized to surface what works, which means they tend to obscure what doesn't, especially at the boundaries of model reliability.

Human oversight from outside the primary optimization loop surfaces these gaps. It also builds the institutional trust that lets organizations move faster on AI adoption, because the measurement infrastructure exists to catch failures early rather than at scale.

This function doesn't disappear as models improve. It evolves in form and focus, from catching factual errors to evaluating reasoning quality, from single-model audits to cross-model governance. The need for structured, honest measurement is durable.
