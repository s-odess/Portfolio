02

Case Study: AI Quality Assurance & Safety Systems

On my own initiative, I designed and executed a structured QA methodology to audit model-assisted publishing workflows at a time when leadership had largely concluded that LLM-generated outputs were production-ready without systematic verification.

Working without dedicated bandwidth or tooling support, I developed custom bash and macOS scripts to capture and catalog model-generated errors via hotkey-triggered screenshots, building a lightweight but reproducible audit trail across two high-risk output categories: privacy violations and factual inaccuracy.

The audit surfaced a 10% model error rate in these categories — failures that the existing automated evaluation pipeline was not designed to detect, because it measured fluency and formatting rather than ground-truth accuracy. This was adversarial auditing in the practical sense: I was testing assumptions the system had been built to confirm, not challenge.

Management acknowledged the findings, which directly informed subsequent QA calibration. Continued measurement was not possible as dedicated audit infrastructure was never allocated, but the core finding remained: production-level model confidence had outpaced production-level model reliability.

Perspective

Scaling systems and effectively auditing them require different approaches and perspectives. In production environments, I have seen that certain important failure modes are not always visible to a system’s internal evaluation mechanisms.

Applying human oversight from outside the primary optimization loop can help surface and address these gaps. My experience indicates this function remains important even as models improve — it simply evolves in form and focus.
