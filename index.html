05

Perspective

Scale is a legitimate business imperative. But scaling a system is not the same as auditing it. In my experience those are distinct functions that require distinct perspectives.

Engineering roles are scoped to build and maintain systems. Oversight requires applying review from outside the system being evaluated, including the perspective of the people the system affects. In the workflows I audited, the most consequential failure modes were not visible to the automated evaluation pipeline. They required a reviewer who was not optimizing toward the same outputs as the system itself.

I have measured this in one production environment. My position is that the oversight requirement is structural, not transitional. It does not diminish as models improve. It changes shape.
